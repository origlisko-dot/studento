# Privacy Policy — Telegram TV Cast

_Last updated: 2026-06-30_

Telegram TV Cast ("the app") lets you play videos on your TV by sending them to a
Telegram bot. This policy explains what data is involved.

## What the app stores on your device

- A randomly generated **device ID** and a temporary **pairing code**, used to
  link your TV to your Telegram chat. No name, email, or account is required.

The app does **not** request location, contacts, camera, microphone, or
advertising identifiers. It requests only the `INTERNET` permission.

## What the backend service processes

When you pair and cast, the backend service operated by the app operator
processes:

- Your **Telegram chat ID** (to know which TV to send your video to) and the
  **pairing code** you send.
- The **video link or file** you send to the bot. Direct links are passed to your
  TV as-is. Telegram-hosted files are streamed through the service to your TV at
  playback time.

This data is held **only transiently** to deliver playback. Pairing records and
temporary media references expire automatically (unpaired codes within minutes,
media references within hours). The service does not build advertising profiles
and does not sell personal data.

## Third parties

- **Telegram** delivers your messages to the bot under
  [Telegram's Privacy Policy](https://telegram.org/privacy).
- The backend runs on the app operator's chosen hosting provider.

## Data retention & deletion

Because the service keeps only transient routing/playback data, stopping use and
deleting the chat with the bot removes the association. To request deletion of
any residual data, contact the operator below.

## Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 and collects no personal profiles.
